Introduction

Roaring  20's  in Paris—a  dazzling  epicenter  of  art,  creativity,  and  rebellion.  Amidst  the extravagant  cabarets, smoky  studios, and  streets alive
with  revolution, one name stood  above all—Kiki, "Queen of Montparnasse" Kiki embodied  the Montparnasse Era,  reaching iconic status.  Kiki
was a force of nature, credited for being the “essential interlocutor” for two generations of French Masters.

From the tender age of 16, Alice Prin became the inspiration and favorite muse/nude model for many of the greatest artists of all time, Muse of a
Thousand Faces. Her titles, Kiki de Montparnasse and KIKI, “Queen of Montparnasse” were bestowed on her after meeting and falling in love with
Man Ray in 1922. Kiki was Modigliani’s muse, the model behind Fujita’s iconic nude portraits, and the subject of Le Violon d’Ingres, Man Rays’
world-famous photograph. The most expensive photo ever sold, an astonishing $12.4 million at Christie's in 2022, (above left).  Her likeness and
namesake were the inspiration behind a successful line of French lingerie, Kiki de Montparnasse - Luxury Lingerie & Related Goods – The Brand

But her story goes far beyond the canvas or camera lens—Kiki was much more than an artist muse/model, she had a command over the avant-
garde world in ways few ever could. She was a singer, dancer, cabaret performer, ﬁlm actress, memoirist, and artist in her own right, compared
to Matisse. In 1928 Ernest Hemingway, captivated by her charisma, wrote the forward to Kiki's Memoirs one of two in his lifetime, Memoirs II.
Hemingway wrote "Kiki dominated the Era of Montparnasse more than Queen Victoria ever dominated the Victorian Era”. The two also shared a
brief aﬀair. Kiki maintains a cultish-like following around the world with over a million super-fans, but her life and legacy have largely been erased
from history…
