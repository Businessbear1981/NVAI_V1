# Auction Meal Fulfillment — Wine Merchant & Basket Curator Research

**Date:** 2026-05-29
**Purpose:** Source partners to fulfill the $495 French Picnic and $795 three-course dinner for NVAI auction events, with one-week lead time from order to delivery to the bidder's address.
**Constraint:** Web access denied in this session — every claim below is from training knowledge through January 2026. Confirm current pricing, inventory, and state-shipping coverage before signing any agreement.

---

## 1. The fulfillment problem in one paragraph

The $495 picnic basket bundles two French wines (Chardonnay + Beaujolais) with three French cheeses, Jamón Ibérico, Jordan almonds, baguette, fig jam, cornichons. The $795 three-course needs hot/chilled food prepared the day-of plus paired wines. Both must arrive at the bidder's residence — wherever in the US — at least 24 hours before the auction begins (to handle wine temperature recovery and cheese tempering). That means **placing the order a week ahead, shipping cold-chain, and white-glove unboxing**. No single national vendor does the entire bundle end-to-end at the right price point. The realistic answer is **one wine merchant + one specialty food curator + one delivery layer** stitched together by the concierge.

### Tier 2 — Local catering for the three-course dinner

The hot three-course dinner cannot be shipped — it has to be cooked locally to the bidder, delivered hot/cold-chain. This is where the architecture forks:

- For **Napa Valley** bidders (Villa Monticello vicinity): partner with **CIA Copia (Culinary Institute of America at Copia)** for white-glove plated delivery, or **PRESS Restaurant (St. Helena)** for on-property prep + local courier.
- For **major-metro** bidders (NYC, LA, Chicago, Miami): use **Cater2.me** or **ezCater** as the booking layer to reach a curated list of premium caterers; NVAI pre-approves one signature menu per city.
- For **everywhere else**: ship the chilled three-course kit from a national premium meal vendor like **Goldbelly**, **CookUnity Premium**, or **Williams Sonoma Holiday Menus** (cooked, then chilled, then shipped, with reheat instructions and a concierge calling the bidder to coordinate plating). Not as elegant; only for cities outside the partner network.

- **Cross-state orders** — K&L (wine, separate package) + Murray's + La Tienda (basket consolidated, two-day FedEx Cold) + a chilled three-course kit if the dinner SKU is selected.

## 5. State-by-state wine shipping reality check

Direct-to-consumer wine shipping is governed at the state level. The numbers move yearly. Categories as of late 2025:

- **Open shipping (~36 states)**: CA, NY, FL, TX, IL, OR, WA, MA, CO, AZ, NV, GA, NC, VA, etc.
- **License-required (~6 states)**: KY, OH, etc. — requires a fulfillment-house license, adds cost.
- **Prohibited (~5 states)**: UT, MS, AL, RI, DE — bidders in these states cannot legally receive shipped wine.

NVAI must geo-gate the picnic offering to legal states, and offer a "wine credit" or non-wine alternative for bidders in prohibited states.

## 6. Recommended plan

### Operating model

1. **NVAI concierge places the order on the bidder's behalf** — bidder doesn't shop. They pick a SKU at registration, give a delivery address, and the concierge handles fulfillment. Order goes in **7 days before the auction**.
2. **Two SKUs**, both already in `backend/data/auction-meals.json`:
   - **French Picnic — $495** (chilled basket, cross-country friendly)
   - **Three-Course Dinner — $795** (locally plated, requires same-metro partner)
3. **Single-bill experience** for the bidder — NVAI invoices them through Stripe at the same time as the auction registration. NVAI then pays the vendors directly.
