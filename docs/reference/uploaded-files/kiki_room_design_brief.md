# Kiki de Montparnasse - Moulin Rouge Room Design Brief

## The Story
**Kiki (Alice Prin)** - "Queen of Montparnasse" (1901-1953)
- Born in Châtillon-sur-Seine in abject poverty
- Came to Paris at age 12
- Became a symbol of 1920s Paris bohemian culture
- Muse, model, painter, dancer, cabaret performer
- Relationship with Man Ray (1921-1928) - hundreds of portraits, 9 films
- Crowned "Queen of Montparnasse" at age 28 in 1929
- Owned her own cabaret "Chez Kiki"
- Died April 29, 1953 at age 51 from alcoholism and drug abuse
- Buried in Cimetière Parisien de Thiais with huge crowd of artists and admirers

## Visual Aesthetic Elements for Room

### Color Palette
- Deep cabaret red velvet
- Black and white photography (1920s era)
- Gold accents (makeup, jewelry)
- Magenta/purple frames and borders
- Cream/beige tones (vintage photographs)

### Key Visual References
1. **Kiki's Makeup** - Bold, artistic makeup with:
   - Kohl-lined eyes
   - Red lips (scarlet emphasis)
   - Painted eyebrows
   - Translucent skin as canvas
   
2. **La Rotonde Cabaret** - Where she performed
   - Red signage
   - Parisian street-level venue
   - Intimate performance space

3. **Moulin Rouge** - The iconic cabaret
   - Red windmill
   - Can-can dancers
   - Belle Époque aesthetic
   - Theatrical staging

4. **Man Ray Photographs**
   - "Le Violon d'Ingres" (1924) - Most iconic ($12.4M highest price ever paid for a photograph)
   - Portrait series
   - Experimental photography
   - Black and white with artistic manipulation

5. **Modigliani Portrait** - "Seated Woman with Blue Dress" (1917-19)
   - Alice Prin (Kiki) before she became famous
   - Blue dress
   - Modigliani's characteristic elongated features
   - Melancholic expression
