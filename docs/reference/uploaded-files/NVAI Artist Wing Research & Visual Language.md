# NVAI Artist Wing Research & Visual Language

## 1. Picasso: The Evolution of Genius
### Wing 1: Le Bateau-Lavoir (1901 - Blue Period)
- **Historical Context:** A run-down tenement in Montmartre, Paris. The "Laundry Boat" was the birthplace of modern art.
- **Visual Language:** Candlelit gloom, narrow garrets, monochromatic blue palettes, themes of poverty and melancholy.
- **Key Figures:** Casagemas (his friend whose suicide triggered the Blue Period).
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A cramped, dimly lit studio with peeling wallpaper and the smell of turpentine.

### Wing 2: The Atelier (1934-1967 - Late Periods)
- **Historical Context:** His later years in the South of France (Vallauris, Mougins).
- **Visual Language:** Bright Mediterranean light, terracotta tiles, ceramic vessels, vibrant colors, expressive brushwork.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A sun-drenched, spacious studio filled with canvases, pottery, and the clutter of a prolific master.

## 2. Chagall: The Dreamer's Journey
- **Historical Context:** From the shtetls of Vitebsk to the sun-soaked Saint-Paul-de-Vence.
- **Visual Language:** Dreamlike, floating figures, deep ultramarine and crimson, stained-glass light effects.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A space that feels like a floating dream, with motifs of fiddlers, lovers, and animals.

## 3. Modigliani & Kiki de Montparnasse: The Roaring 20s
- **Historical Context:** 1920s Montparnasse, the heart of bohemian Paris.
- **Visual Language:** Café culture (Café du Dôme, La Rotonde), smoke-filled rooms, jazz, elongated portraits.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A 1920s Parisian café/cabaret with velvet curtains, phonographs, and the atmosphere of a "lost generation."

## 4. The Italian Masters: Da Vinci & Raphael
- **Historical Context:** The Sforza Court in Milan (Da Vinci) and High Renaissance Florence (Raphael).
- **Visual Language:** Florentine workshops, sepia drafting paper, ribbed-vault ceilings, candlelight, linseed oil.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A Renaissance "studiolo" or workshop with stone arches and scholarly artifacts.

## 5. Matisse: The Pictogram Signature
- **Historical Context:** Late career in Vence, specifically the Chapelle du Rosaire.
- **Visual Language:** Paper cutouts, bold primary colors (cobalt, lemon, vermilion), white walls, minimalist lines.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A bright, airy chapel-like space with large-scale cutouts and stained-glass light.

## 6. Kandinsky: The Bauhaus & Murnau
- **Historical Context:** The Bauhaus school in Weimar and the Bavarian town of Murnau.
- **Visual Language:** Geometric abstraction, primary colors, white walls, musicality in visual form.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A clean, modernist Bauhaus interior with a focus on form and function.

## 7. Frida Kahlo: Casa Azul
- **Historical Context:** Coyoacán, Mexico City. Her lifelong home and sanctuary.
- **Visual Language:** Bright cobalt blue walls, Mexican folk art, pre-Columbian artifacts, lush tropical plants.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** The interior of Casa Azul, vibrant and deeply personal, reflecting her pain and passion.

## 8. Monet: The Giverny Sanctuary
- **Historical Context:** His home and gardens in Giverny, France.
- **Visual Language:** Impressionist light, water lilies, Japanese bridges, lush greenery, soft pastels.
- **Aesthetic Anchor:** A "conservation lab" set within a garden pavilion, emphasizing the delicate nature of his late works.
